BIOGRAPHY

My career began with an administrative position in an engineering firm. A competing firm created a position for a proposal coordinator, for which I applied and was hired. I stayed in similar marketing and business development roles of increasing responsibility. After the death of my husband, I left my role at WSP to pursue self-employment in Calgary. I worked in Calgary for a few years until accepting a role at Global eTraining as an account manager. My experience with engineering firms was useful there, since their largest clients were in engineering. After two years at Global eTraining I returned to Manitoba briefly, then moved to Ottawa. There I revived my private consulting practice and later decided to return to school at Georgian College.
